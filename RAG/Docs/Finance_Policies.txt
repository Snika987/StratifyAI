Finance Operations and Governance Policy

Effective Date: 01 April 2026
Owner: Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
Headquarters: Bengaluru, India

1. Purpose

1.1 The purpose of this Finance Operations and Governance Policy (“Policy”) is to establish a standardized, controlled, and auditable framework for financial management across ApexNova Technologies (“ApexNova”), ensuring fiscal discipline, regulatory compliance, transparency, and operational efficiency.

1.2 This Policy defines the authority limits, financial controls, accounting standards, reporting requirements, service level commitments, and escalation mechanisms governing the Finance Department.

1.3 The Policy is structured to:
a. Safeguard company assets.
b. Ensure compliance with Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS), Companies Act 2013, Income Tax Act, GST regulations, and applicable international regulations for global client operations.
c. Standardize financial processes across business units and geographies.
d. Establish approval hierarchies with defined monetary thresholds.
e. Enable audit readiness and support enterprise knowledge systems including Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) frameworks.

2. Scope

2.1 This Policy applies to:
a. All Finance Department employees.
b. Business Unit (BU) Heads with budget authority.
c. Procurement, HR, Sales, Legal, and Operations teams interacting with Finance.
d. Subsidiaries and branch offices operating under ApexNova.
e. Third-party financial service providers, auditors, consultants, and banking partners.

2.2 Functional Areas Covered:
a. Financial Planning and Analysis (FP&A).
b. Budgeting and Forecasting.
c. Accounts Payable (AP).
d. Accounts Receivable (AR).
e. General Ledger (GL) Accounting.
f. Taxation (Direct and Indirect).
g. Treasury and Cash Management.
h. Fixed Assets Management.
i. Financial Reporting.
j. Statutory Compliance.
k. Internal Controls and Audit Support.
l. Expense Reimbursement.
m. Capital Expenditure (CapEx) Governance.

2.3 This Policy does not supersede statutory obligations. In case of conflict, statutory law prevails.

3. Definitions

3.1 “ApexNova” refers to ApexNova Technologies and its subsidiaries.

3.2 “CFO” refers to Chief Financial Officer.

3.3 “Controller” refers to Financial Controller responsible for accounting oversight.

3.4 “Budget Owner” refers to an executive or manager responsible for cost center budgets.

3.5 “Monetary Authority Limit (MAL)” refers to maximum financial approval authority.

3.6 “CapEx” refers to capital expenditure for assets exceeding INR 1,00,000 with useful life greater than 12 months.

3.7 “OpEx” refers to operational expenditure.

3.8 “Material Variance” refers to deviation exceeding 5 percent of approved budget or INR 25,00,000, whichever is lower.

3.9 “Working Capital” refers to current assets minus current liabilities.

3.10 “ERP System” refers to ApexNova’s enterprise resource planning platform.

3.11 “Three-Way Match” refers to validation of purchase order, goods receipt, and invoice prior to payment.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

4.1 Board of Directors
a. Approve annual financial statements.
b. Approve capital investments above INR 25,00,00,000.
c. Oversee financial risk management.

4.2 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
a. Approve annual operating plan.
b. Approve unbudgeted expenditure above INR 5,00,00,000.
c. Review quarterly financial performance.

4.3 Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
a. Own and enforce this Policy.
b. Approve capital projects up to INR 25,00,00,000.
c. Approve write-offs above INR 50,00,000.
d. Ensure regulatory compliance.
e. Present financial reports to Board.

4.4 Financial Controller
a. Ensure accounting accuracy.
b. Supervise GL, AP, AR.
c. Approve journal entries above INR 10,00,000.
d. Lead statutory audit coordination.

4.5 Finance Managers
a. Monitor budget utilization.
b. Approve vendor payments up to INR 25,00,000.
c. Review monthly reconciliations.

4.6 Treasury Manager
a. Manage banking relationships.
b. Monitor daily cash position.
c. Execute foreign exchange hedging within approved limits.

4.7 Budget Owners
a. Ensure expenditures remain within approved budgets.
b. Provide variance explanations.
c. Initiate procurement requests.

4.8 Internal Audit
a. Conduct periodic financial control audits.
b. Report control deficiencies to Audit Committee.

5. Policy Statements

5.1 Budgeting
a. Annual budget cycle begins in November and concludes by 31 January.
b. All BUs must submit detailed budget proposals including headcount, revenue projections, and cost breakdowns.
c. Budget growth above 15 percent year-on-year requires CFO review.

5.2 Expenditure Authorization
a. No expenditure shall be incurred without approved budget allocation.
b. Unbudgeted OpEx above INR 10,00,000 requires CFO approval.
c. Split invoicing to circumvent approval thresholds is prohibited.

5.3 Capital Expenditure
a. CapEx requires business case with ROI analysis.
b. Projects exceeding INR 1,00,00,000 require IRR minimum of 15 percent.
c. CapEx above INR 5,00,00,000 requires CEO concurrence.

5.4 Procurement and Payment
a. All purchases above INR 50,000 require purchase order.
b. Three-way match mandatory before payment.
c. Payment terms standard: 30 days unless negotiated.

5.5 Revenue Recognition
a. Revenue recognized per Ind AS 115.
b. Milestone-based contracts require documented acceptance evidence.
c. Deferred revenue accounted monthly.

5.6 Accounts Receivable
a. Standard credit terms: 30 days.
b. Overdue above 60 days escalated to BU Head.
c. Provision for doubtful debts applied after 90 days overdue.

5.7 Expense Reimbursement
a. Claims must be submitted within 30 days of expense.
b. Supporting documentation mandatory.
c. Claims above INR 1,00,000 require Finance Manager review.

5.8 Write-offs
a. AR write-offs up to INR 10,00,000: Finance Manager.
b. INR 10,00,001 to 50,00,000: Controller.
c. Above INR 50,00,000: CFO.

6. Operational Procedures

6.1 Budget Preparation Workflow
Step 1: FP&A distributes templates.
Step 2: BU submits projections.
Step 3: Finance reviews assumptions.
Step 4: Consolidation and variance analysis.
Step 5: CFO review and iteration.
Step 6: CEO approval.
Step 7: Board presentation.

6.2 Accounts Payable Process
a. Vendor registration with KYC validation.
b. Invoice submission to centralized AP mailbox.
c. ERP entry within 2 working days.
d. Three-way match verification.
e. Payment run executed weekly.

6.3 Accounts Receivable Process
a. Invoice generated within 2 working days of milestone completion.
b. Monthly AR aging review.
c. Follow-up cycle at 15, 30, 45 days overdue.
d. Legal notice after 90 days.

6.4 Monthly Close Process
a. Close calendar issued monthly.
b. Journal entries cut-off: last working day.
c. Reconciliations completed within 5 working days.
d. Financial statements prepared within 7 working days.

6.5 Treasury Operations
a. Daily cash position review.
b. Minimum liquidity buffer: 3 months fixed operating expenses.
c. Surplus investment per approved investment policy.

7. Approval Matrix

7.1 Operational Expenditure
Up to INR 5,00,000: Budget Owner
INR 5,00,001 to 25,00,000: Finance Manager
INR 25,00,001 to 1,00,00,000: Controller
Above INR 1,00,00,000: CFO

7.2 Capital Expenditure
Up to INR 50,00,000: Finance Manager + BU Head
INR 50,00,001 to 5,00,00,000: CFO
Above INR 5,00,00,000: CEO + CFO

7.3 Contract Approvals
Revenue contracts above INR 10,00,00,000 require CFO review before execution.

8. Escalation Process

8.1 Level 1: Finance Manager
Response time: 2 working days.

8.2 Level 2: Financial Controller
Response time: 3 working days.

8.3 Level 3: CFO
Response time: 5 working days.

8.4 Level 4: Audit Committee
Triggered for suspected fraud above INR 1,00,00,000 or material compliance breach.

9. SLA Guidelines

9.1 Vendor Payment Processing
Within 7 working days from validated invoice.

9.2 Expense Reimbursement
Within 10 working days from approval.

9.3 Invoice Generation
Within 2 working days of revenue trigger.

9.4 Monthly Financial Reporting
Within 7 working days after month-end.

9.5 Budget Variance Reporting
Within 5 working days post month-end.

10. Exception Handling

10.1 Emergency Procurement
a. Justification required.
b. Post-facto PO within 3 working days.
c. CFO notification if amount exceeds INR 20,00,000.

10.2 Budget Overrun
a. Variance above 5 percent requires written explanation.
b. Reforecast submission within 10 working days.

10.3 Payment Term Exceptions
a. Advance payment above INR 10,00,000 requires CFO approval.
b. Performance bank guarantee recommended for advances exceeding INR 50,00,000.

11. Compliance & Audit Controls

11.1 Statutory Audit conducted annually.

11.2 Internal audit conducted quarterly.

11.3 GST returns filed monthly before statutory deadlines.

11.4 TDS returns filed quarterly.

11.5 Segregation of duties enforced in ERP.

11.6 Audit Trail
a. Journal entry logs retained for 8 years.
b. Supporting documents archived digitally.

11.7 Fraud Reporting Mechanism
Anonymous whistleblower channel monitored by Audit Committee.

12. Security Considerations

12.1 Financial data stored in encrypted servers.

12.2 Bank access restricted via dual authentication.

12.3 Payment file approval requires maker-checker control.

12.4 ERP access controlled by role-based permissions.

12.5 Data exports above INR 10,00,00,000 financial data volume require CFO approval.

14. FAQs

14.1 What is the threshold for requiring a purchase order?
All purchases above INR 50,000 require PO.

14.2 How are unbudgeted expenses handled?
CFO approval required for amounts above INR 10,00,000.

14.3 What is the timeline for expense reimbursement?
Within 10 working days of approval.

14.4 How are doubtful debts treated?
Provision created after 90 days overdue; escalation to BU Head.

14.5 Can advance payments be made to vendors?
Yes, with CFO approval above INR 10,00,000 and appropriate safeguards.

14.6 What is considered material variance?
Deviation exceeding 5 percent of budget or INR 25,00,000, whichever is lower.
