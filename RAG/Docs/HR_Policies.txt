ApexNova Technologies
Human Resources Operations and Governance Policy
Document ID: AN-HR-OPG-001
Effective Date: 01 April 2026
Owner: Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)
Headquarters: Bengaluru, India

1. Purpose

1.1 The purpose of this Human Resources Operations and Governance Policy (“Policy”) is to establish a comprehensive framework governing HR processes, decision-making authorities, service delivery standards, compliance controls, and operational accountability within ApexNova Technologies (“ApexNova”).

1.2 This Policy is designed to:
a. Standardize HR processes across all business units and global locations.
b. Ensure compliance with applicable labor laws and regulatory requirements.
c. Define roles, responsibilities, approval thresholds, and escalation mechanisms.
d. Establish measurable service levels and reporting metrics.
e. Enable operational consistency to support a scalable workforce between 1000 and 2500 employees.
f. Provide structured documentation to support knowledge management systems and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) solutions.

1.3 This Policy applies to all HR functions including Talent Acquisition, Compensation & Benefits, Payroll, Employee Relations, Learning & Development, HR Operations, Workforce Planning, and Global Mobility.

2. Scope

2.1 This Policy applies to:
a. All permanent, probationary, contractual, and temporary employees of ApexNova.
b. All HR personnel and line managers involved in people-related decisions.
c. All domestic operations in India and international client-facing operations.
d. Third-party service providers engaged in payroll processing, benefits administration, recruitment, background verification, and HR technology platforms.

2.2 This Policy governs the following HR domains:
a. Recruitment and onboarding.
b. Compensation administration and salary revisions.
c. Payroll processing and benefits management.
d. Leave management and attendance.
e. Performance management.
f. Disciplinary actions and terminations.
g. Workforce restructuring and redundancies.
h. Employee grievance handling.
i. HR data management and reporting.

2.3 This Policy does not override statutory labor laws. Where local legislation conflicts with this Policy, statutory requirements shall prevail.

3. Definitions

3.1 “ApexNova” refers to ApexNova Technologies and its subsidiaries.

3.2 “HR” refers to the Human Resources Department.

3.3 “CHRO” refers to the Chief Human Resources Officer.

3.4 “HRBP” refers to HR Business Partner assigned to specific business units.

3.5 “Business Unit (BU)” refers to any defined operational or functional division.

3.6 “Monetary Authority Limit (MAL)” refers to the maximum financial amount an individual role can approve.

3.7 “Employee” refers to any individual on ApexNova payroll.

3.8 “Contractor” refers to any non-payroll resource engaged via third-party agreement.

3.9 “SLA” refers to Service Level Agreement.

3.10 “Grievance” refers to a formal complaint submitted by an employee regarding workplace matters.

3.11 “Gross Misconduct” refers to severe violations including but not limited to fraud, data theft, harassment, violence, or breach of confidentiality.

3.12 “Confidential HR Data” includes personal identification information, compensation data, disciplinary records, medical records, and performance documentation.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

4.1 Board of Directors
a. Approve executive compensation.
b. Oversee compliance governance.
c. Review workforce risk reports annually.

4.2 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
a. Approve workforce planning budgets.
b. Approve senior leadership hiring above Grade G12.
c. Review HR metrics quarterly.

4.3 Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)
a. Own this Policy and related procedures.
b. Approve salary structures and benefits design.
c. Authorize compensation exceptions exceeding INR 20,00,000 annually.
d. Oversee compliance audits and regulatory reporting.

4.4 HR Business Partners (HRBPs)
a. Support BU heads in workforce planning.
b. Approve compensation adjustments up to INR 5,00,000 annually.
c. Lead grievance resolution within assigned BUs.
d. Ensure adherence to SLAs.

4.5 HR Operations Manager
a. Oversee payroll processing.
b. Ensure onboarding and exit process compliance.
c. Maintain HRIS data integrity.
d. Escalate payroll discrepancies above INR 50,000 to CHRO.

4.6 Talent Acquisition Manager
a. Approve hiring budgets within allocated headcount.
b. Ensure recruitment cycle time adherence.
c. Manage external recruitment vendor contracts up to INR 50,00,000 annually.

4.7 Line Managers
a. Initiate hiring requisitions.
b. Conduct performance reviews.
c. Recommend salary increments within approved budget.

4.8 Finance Department
a. Validate compensation budgets.
b. Approve payroll disbursements.
c. Monitor financial impact of workforce changes.

4.9 Legal Department
a. Review disciplinary cases involving potential litigation.
b. Validate termination processes.
c. Ensure labor law compliance.

5. Policy Statements

5.1 Workforce Planning
a. Annual workforce planning shall be completed by 31 January for the upcoming fiscal year.
b. Headcount growth above 10 percent requires CEO approval.
c. Unplanned hiring exceeding approved headcount must be justified with ROI documentation.

5.2 Recruitment
a. All positions must have an approved requisition ID.
b. Background verification is mandatory for all hires.
c. Offer validity is 7 calendar days unless extended by HR.
d. Hiring above Grade G10 requires CHRO approval.

5.3 Compensation
a. Annual increment cycle shall occur in April.
b. Standard increment range: 6 to 12 percent of fixed pay.
c. Promotions may include 12 to 25 percent adjustment.
d. Compensation exceeding salary band by more than 15 percent requires CHRO and CFO joint approval.

5.4 Payroll
a. Payroll is processed monthly.
b. Salary disbursement occurs on or before the 28th of each month.
c. Payroll variance tolerance threshold is 0.5 percent of total payroll cost.
d. Corrections above INR 25,000 require HR Operations Manager review.

5.5 Leave Management
a. Annual leave entitlement: 18 days.
b. Sick leave: 12 days per year.
c. Carry-forward limit: 10 days.
d. Leave encashment permitted only upon separation.

5.6 Performance Management
a. Annual appraisal cycle runs January to March.
b. Ratings calibrated at BU level.
c. Top performance rating capped at 20 percent of workforce.

5.7 Disciplinary Action
a. Progressive discipline approach: verbal warning, written warning, final warning, termination.
b. Gross misconduct may lead to immediate suspension pending investigation.
c. Investigation completion timeline: 15 working days.

5.8 Termination
a. Notice period: 60 days for mid-level employees; 90 days for senior leadership.
b. Buyout permitted subject to manager and HR approval.
c. Final settlement processed within 30 days.

6. Operational Procedures

6.1 Hiring Workflow
Step 1: Manager submits requisition in HRIS.
Step 2: HRBP reviews role justification within 2 working days.
Step 3: Finance validates budget within 3 working days.
Step 4: Talent Acquisition initiates sourcing.
Step 5: Interview panel conducts structured evaluation.
Step 6: Compensation benchmarking performed.
Step 7: Offer approval based on approval matrix.
Step 8: Background verification initiated.
Step 9: Onboarding scheduled.

6.2 Payroll Processing Workflow
a. Attendance data freeze: 20th of each month.
b. Variable pay inputs deadline: 22nd.
c. Payroll draft shared with Finance: 24th.
d. Final approval: 26th.
e. Bank transfer file submission: 27th.

6.3 Grievance Handling Procedure
a. Complaint logged via HR portal.
b. Acknowledgment within 2 working days.
c. Preliminary review within 5 working days.
d. Investigation within 15 working days.
e. Closure report issued within 3 days of decision.

6.4 Exit Process
a. Resignation recorded in HRIS.
b. Knowledge transfer plan documented.
c. IT asset recovery initiated.
d. Clearance from Finance and Admin.
e. Full and final settlement calculated.

7. Approval Matrix

7.1 Hiring Approval
a. Entry-level: HRBP + BU Head.
b. Mid-level: HRBP + BU Head + Finance.
c. Senior-level: CHRO + CEO.

7.2 Salary Adjustments
a. Up to INR 5,00,000 annually: HRBP.
b. INR 5,00,001 to 20,00,000: CHRO.
c. Above INR 20,00,000: CHRO + CFO.

7.3 Severance
a. Statutory only: HRBP.
b. Up to 3 months additional pay: CHRO.
c. Above 3 months: CEO approval.

8. Escalation Process

8.1 Level 1: HRBP
Response time: 2 working days.

8.2 Level 2: HR Operations Manager or Talent Acquisition Manager
Response time: 3 working days.

8.3 Level 3: CHRO
Response time: 5 working days.

8.4 Level 4: CEO
Reserved for high-risk cases involving legal exposure above INR 1,00,00,000.

9. SLA Guidelines

9.1 Recruitment
a. Standard positions: 45 calendar days.
b. Niche roles: 60 calendar days.

9.2 Offer Letter Release
Within 3 working days of final approval.

9.3 Payroll Queries
Response within 2 working days.
Resolution within 5 working days.

9.4 Grievance Resolution
Within 30 calendar days.

9.5 Exit Settlement
Within 30 days of last working day.

10. Exception Handling

10.1 All exceptions must be documented in writing.

10.2 Compensation exceptions beyond band require:
a. Market benchmarking data.
b. Business justification.
c. CHRO approval.

10.3 Emergency Hiring Exception
a. Critical client-driven requirement.
b. CEO waiver of standard requisition timeline.
c. Post-facto documentation within 7 days.

10.4 Policy deviation logs must be reviewed quarterly by HR Governance Committee.

11. Compliance & Audit Controls

11.1 Internal audit of HR processes shall occur biannually.

11.2 Payroll audit sampling rate: minimum 5 percent of workforce.

11.3 Background verification audit compliance threshold: 100 percent.

11.4 Labor law compliance checklist to be validated quarterly.

11.5 Records retention:
a. Employee records: 7 years post separation.
b. Payroll records: 8 years.
c. Investigation records: 10 years.

11.6 Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action against responsible personnel.

12. Security Considerations

12.1 HR data must be stored in encrypted systems.

12.2 Access to compensation data limited to HR, Finance, and authorized leadership.

12.3 Role-based access controls implemented in HRIS.

12.4 Data export above 500 employee records requires CHRO approval.

12.5 All HR staff must complete annual data privacy training.

12.6 Cross-border data transfers must comply with applicable data protection laws.

13. Reporting & Metrics

13.1 Monthly HR Dashboard Metrics:
a. Headcount variance.
b. Attrition rate.
c. Offer acceptance rate.
d. Recruitment cycle time.
e. Payroll accuracy percentage.
f. Training hours per employee.
g. Grievance resolution time.

13.2 Quarterly Reports:
a. Diversity ratio.
b. Compensation ratio analysis.
c. Overtime expenditure.
d. Absenteeism rate.

13.3 Annual Reports:
a. Workforce cost as percentage of revenue.
b. Voluntary vs involuntary attrition.
c. Employee engagement survey index.

13.4 Target Benchmarks:
a. Attrition rate below 15 percent annually.
b. Payroll accuracy above 99.5 percent.
c. Recruitment cycle time adherence 90 percent.

14. FAQs

14.1 What is the approval process for urgent hiring?
Urgent hiring requires BU Head justification and CHRO approval. Documentation must be completed within 7 days.

14.2 Can compensation exceed the defined band?
Yes, with documented market justification and CHRO approval. Exceeding 15 percent above band requires CFO concurrence.

14.3 How long does payroll correction take?
Typically 5 working days after issue validation.

14.4 What is the process for handling harassment complaints?
Immediate acknowledgment, confidential investigation within 15 working days, and compliance with applicable workplace laws.

14.5 Can notice period be reduced?
Yes, subject to manager, HRBP, and business continuity considerations.

14.6 What happens if SLA targets are not met?
Root cause analysis is conducted. Repeated breaches may impact performance ratings of responsible managers.

14.7 How are contractor conversions handled?
Subject to workforce planning approval and revised compensation benchmarking.
