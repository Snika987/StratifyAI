ApexNova Technologies
Information Technology (IT) Operations, Hardware, Software and Infrastructure Governance Policy
Document ID: AN-IT-OPG-001
Effective Date: 01 April 2026
Owner: Chief Information Officer (CIO)
Headquarters: Bengaluru, India

1. Purpose

1.1 The purpose of this Information Technology (IT) Operations, Hardware, Software and Infrastructure Governance Policy (“Policy”) is to establish a unified governance framework for managing all IT assets, platforms, applications, infrastructure, and technology services across ApexNova Technologies (“ApexNova”).

1.2 This Policy ensures:
a. High availability, reliability, and scalability of IT systems supporting global client operations.
b. Protection of company data and digital assets.
c. Standardized operational controls across software, hardware, networks, and cloud environments.
d. Clear authority levels for approvals, procurement, and change management.
e. Regulatory compliance including data protection and cybersecurity requirements.
f. Structured documentation suitable for enterprise knowledge systems and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) use cases.

1.3 This Policy applies to IT teams managing software development platforms, enterprise applications, end-user computing devices, network infrastructure, data centers, cloud services, cybersecurity operations, and IT service management.

2. Scope

2.1 This Policy applies to:
a. All IT Department employees.
b. All employees using ApexNova IT systems.
c. Third-party vendors providing IT hardware, software, SaaS platforms, cloud services, and managed services.
d. All domestic and international offices of ApexNova.

2.2 Domains Covered:
a. IT Service Management (ITSM).
b. Software Development Infrastructure.
c. Enterprise Applications (ERP, HRIS, CRM).
d. Cloud Infrastructure (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS).
e. On-Premise Data Center Infrastructure.
f. Network and Security Infrastructure.
g. End-User Computing (EUC).
h. IT Asset Management.
i. Change and Release Management.
j. Incident and Problem Management.
k. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery.
l. Vendor and License Management.

2.3 This Policy covers both capital and operational expenditures relating to IT.

3. Definitions

3.1 “ApexNova” refers to ApexNova Technologies and its subsidiaries.

3.2 “CIO” refers to Chief Information Officer.

3.3 “ITSM” refers to IT Service Management.

3.4 “Incident” refers to any unplanned interruption or degradation of IT services.

3.5 “Major Incident (P1)” refers to critical service disruption affecting revenue, security, or more than 25 percent of employees.

3.6 “Change Request (CR)” refers to a formal request to modify IT systems.

3.7 “Standard Change” refers to pre-approved low-risk change.

3.8 “Emergency Change” refers to urgent change required to prevent major outage or security risk.

3.9 “CapEx” refers to capital expenditure exceeding INR 1,00,000 per asset.

3.10 “Configuration Management Database (CMDB)” refers to centralized IT asset repository.

3.11 “RTO” refers to Recovery Time Objective.

3.12 “RPO” refers to Recovery Point Objective.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

4.1 Board of Directors
a. Approve IT investments above INR 25,00,00,000.
b. Review cybersecurity posture annually.

4.2 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
a. Approve strategic IT initiatives above INR 5,00,00,000.
b. Review quarterly IT performance metrics.

4.3 Chief Information Officer (CIO)
a. Own and enforce this Policy.
b. Approve IT budgets.
c. Authorize IT procurement up to INR 25,00,00,000.
d. Oversee cybersecurity, infrastructure, and software governance.

4.4 IT Infrastructure Head
a. Manage data centers, networks, and cloud environments.
b. Ensure uptime SLAs.
c. Approve hardware procurement up to INR 1,00,00,000.

4.5 IT Applications Head
a. Manage enterprise applications.
b. Approve software licensing within budget.
c. Ensure release management compliance.

4.6 IT Security Manager
a. Lead vulnerability management.
b. Approve access control exceptions.
c. Investigate security incidents.

4.7 IT Service Desk Manager
a. Manage ticketing system.
b. Ensure SLA adherence.
c. Escalate unresolved incidents.

4.8 Asset Management Team
a. Maintain CMDB accuracy.
b. Conduct quarterly asset audits.

4.9 End Users
a. Use IT assets responsibly.
b. Report incidents promptly.

5. Policy Statements

5.1 IT Budgeting
a. Annual IT budget prepared by 31 January.
b. Unplanned expenditure above 10 percent of approved budget requires CIO approval.
c. IT budget variance above 5 percent requires justification.

5.2 Hardware Procurement
a. All hardware purchases above INR 50,000 require CIO or delegate approval.
b. Standard laptop configuration defined annually.
c. Asset tagging mandatory within 48 hours of receipt.
d. Warranty minimum 3 years for laptops and servers.

5.3 Software Licensing
a. All software must be approved by IT Applications Head.
b. Unauthorized installations prohibited.
c. Enterprise license agreements preferred over individual subscriptions.
d. License utilization reviewed quarterly.

5.4 Cloud Infrastructure
a. Cloud resources provisioned via approved cloud accounts only.
b. Cost monitoring conducted weekly.
c. Unused resources must be decommissioned within 15 days.
d. Production systems must have minimum 99.5 percent uptime.

5.5 Access Management
a. Role-based access control mandatory.
b. Privileged access requires dual approval.
c. Access review conducted quarterly.
d. Terminated employee access revoked within 4 hours of exit confirmation.

5.6 Change Management
a. All changes must be logged in ITSM tool.
b. CAB (Change Advisory Board) approval required for non-standard changes.
c. Emergency changes reviewed post-implementation within 48 hours.

5.7 Incident Management
a. P1 incidents acknowledged within 15 minutes.
b. Root Cause Analysis (RCA) required within 5 working days.
c. Repeat incidents escalated to CIO.

5.8 Backup and Recovery
a. Daily incremental backups.
b. Weekly full backups.
c. RTO for critical systems: 4 hours.
d. RPO for critical systems: 1 hour.

5.9 IT Asset Lifecycle
a. Laptop refresh cycle: 36 months.
b. Server refresh cycle: 48 to 60 months.
c. Secure disposal mandatory with data wipe certification.

6. Operational Procedures

6.1 Hardware Request Workflow
Step 1: Employee submits request in IT portal.
Step 2: Manager approval within 2 working days.
Step 3: Asset availability check.
Step 4: Procurement if required.
Step 5: Configuration and deployment.
Step 6: Asset record updated in CMDB.

6.2 Incident Management Workflow
a. Ticket logged via portal, email, or phone.
b. Categorization and prioritization.
c. Assignment to resolver group.
d. SLA timer initiated.
e. Resolution or workaround.
f. Closure confirmation.

6.3 Change Management Workflow
a. Change request submission.
b. Risk and impact assessment.
c. CAB review.
d. Implementation scheduling.
e. Testing and validation.
f. Post-implementation review.

6.4 Software Release Process
a. Development completion.
b. Code review and security scan.
c. QA testing.
d. UAT sign-off.
e. Production deployment.
f. Monitoring for 24 hours.

6.5 Asset Disposal Process
a. Approval from IT Infrastructure Head.
b. Data wipe using certified tools.
c. Physical destruction if required.
d. Disposal certificate archived.

7.1 Hardware Procurement

Up to INR 10,00,000 annually: IT Applications Head
INR 10,00,001 to 50,00,000: CIO
Above INR 50,00,000: CIO + CFO

7.3 Cloud Expenditure
Up to INR 25,00,000 monthly: Infrastructure Head
Above INR 25,00,000 monthly: CIO

8. Escalation Process

8.1 Level 1: Service Desk
Response: Immediate logging.

8.2 Level 2: Resolver Group Lead
Response within 2 hours for P1.

8.3 Level 3: IT Infrastructure or Applications Head
Response within 4 hours.

8.4 Level 4: CIO
Escalation for outages exceeding 4 hours or financial impact above INR 50,00,000.

9. SLA Guidelines

9.1 Incident Response
P1: Response 15 minutes; Resolution 4 hours.
P2: Response 1 hour; Resolution 8 hours.
P3: Response 4 hours; Resolution 2 business days.
P4: Response 1 business day; Resolution 5 business days.

9.2 Service Requests
Hardware request fulfillment: 5 working days.
Software access request: 2 working days.

9.3 System Availability
Critical systems: 99.5 percent monthly uptime.
Non-critical systems: 98 percent monthly uptime.

9.4 Backup Verification
Backup integrity test monthly.

10. Exception Handling

10.1 Emergency Change
a. CIO approval required.
b. Documentation within 24 hours.
c. CAB review post-implementation.

10.2 Hardware Shortage
a. Temporary device allocation.
b. Approval for expedited procurement if delay exceeds 5 working days.

10.3 Software Compliance Exception
a. Business justification required.
b. CIO approval.
c. Time-bound approval not exceeding 90 days.

11. Compliance & Audit Controls

11.1 Quarterly IT internal audit.

11.2 Annual external cybersecurity assessment.

11.3 Patch compliance target: 95 percent within 30 days of release.

11.4 Vulnerability scan monthly.

11.5 Asset audit variance tolerance: 1 percent.

11.6 Log retention minimum 1 year for security logs.

12. Security Considerations

12.1 Multi-factor authentication mandatory for remote access.

12.2 Endpoint encryption required for all laptops.

12.3 Network segmentation for production systems.

12.4 Data classification policy enforced.

12.5 Security awareness training mandatory annually.

12.6 Incident reporting within 1 hour of detection.

b. IT risk register review.
c. Disaster recovery test results.

14. FAQs

14.1 How long does laptop provisioning take?
Standard fulfillment within 5 working days.

14.2 What is the response time for critical outages?
15 minutes acknowledgment; 4 hours resolution target.

14.3 Are employees allowed to install personal software?
No. Unauthorized installations are prohibited.

14.4 What is the backup policy?
Daily incremental and weekly full backups with defined RTO and RPO.

14.5 How are emergency changes handled?
CIO approval required with documentation within 24 hours.

14.6 What happens if SLA targets are missed?
Root cause analysis conducted; corrective actions implemented.
